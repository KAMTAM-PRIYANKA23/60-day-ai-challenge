# 🧭 Task Compass

**Day 37 of #60DaysOfAI**

**Task Compass** is a premium, interactive management simulation that helps users understand **how work flows through real organizations**. Rather than testing job titles or memorized knowledge, the application teaches ownership, delegation, collaboration, and workflow thinking through engaging drag-and-drop scenarios.

Built as a **single-file HTML application**, Task Compass runs completely offline using only **HTML, CSS, and Vanilla JavaScript**.

---

# 🎯 Project Objective

Modern organizations succeed because teams work together—not because one person knows everything.

Task Compass helps users discover:

* Who owns different kinds of work
* How tasks move across departments
* Why collaboration matters
* When responsibilities should be delegated
* How communication flows inside organizations

Instead of giving right-or-wrong answers, every challenge provides practical explanations that mirror real workplace decision-making.

# 🎮 Gameplay

The experience is divided into **three progressively challenging stages**, helping players build confidence through guided learning.

---

## 🧩 Stage 1 — Who Owns This?

Players receive realistic workplace tasks such as:

> "A customer reports that payments fail only on iPhones."

They must drag the most appropriate role into the ownership slot.

Possible roles include:

* Frontend Developer
* Backend Developer
* QA Engineer
* Product Manager
* UX Designer
* Customer Support
* Engineering Manager

After each submission, the application explains:

* ✅ Primary Owner
* 🤝 Supporting Roles
* 💡 Why that role typically owns the task

Rather than simply saying "Correct" or "Wrong," the game focuses on workplace reasoning.

---

## 🔄 Stage 2 — Task Routing

Players now manage how work flows through an organization.

Example workflow:

Customer Support → QA → Backend Developer → Product Manager → Customer

Tasks vary in complexity, requiring players to build efficient workflows by arranging role cards in the correct sequence.

Once submitted, the application animates the task moving through the organization and explains why the selected order is commonly used.

---

## 🤝 Stage 3 — Collaboration Challenge

Real workplace problems rarely belong to just one person.

Players analyze larger organizational scenarios such as:

* Customer satisfaction suddenly drops
* Sales increase dramatically
* Negative reviews appear online
* A major feature is delayed

Instead of selecting one owner, users assign up to **four collaborating departments or roles**.

The application then reveals:

* Primary Owner
* Supporting Teams
* Communication Flow
* Collaboration Strategy
* Practical workplace reasoning

# 📊 Learning Progress

* Ownership

# 📈 Final Reflection Dashboard

At the end of the experience, users receive a personalized reflection including:

* What they understood well
* Where they over-assigned responsibility
* Where they underestimated collaboration
* One practical insight about how organizations actually work

Example:

> *"Many real workplace problems are solved by teams rather than individuals."*

A visual radar/bar chart summarizes learning across all four competency areas using only HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
