Day 21 – Digital Privacy Intelligence Dashboard
Overview

Today I built a Digital Privacy Intelligence Dashboard, an interactive web application designed to visualize a user's digital footprint, privacy exposure, and potential data collection risks across commonly used online platforms.

The dashboard transforms a list of digital services into meaningful privacy analytics, helping users better understand how their online activities may contribute to their overall digital presence.

Objective

The goal of this project was to:

Visualize digital footprint exposure
Analyze privacy risks and tracking surfaces
Understand ecosystem concentration across companies
Explore digital twin profiling concepts
Present privacy insights through modern dashboard design
Practice advanced frontend development and data visualization
Features Implemented
Digital Footprint Score

Calculated an overall score representing the extent of digital presence based on the services used.

Privacy Score

Estimated privacy protection level based on platform exposure and ecosystem concentration.

Exposure Heatmap

Visualized estimated exposure levels across different categories of personal data.

Company Exposure Ranking

Ranked organizations based on their potential access to user-generated data across services.

Data Collection Matrix

Displayed possible categories of information that services may collect.

Risk Radar

Provided a visual representation of different privacy-related risk dimensions.

Digital Twin Profile

Generated estimated behavioral and demographic insights while clearly separating Facts from Estimates.

Most Valuable Data Assets

Highlighted the types of information that may be most valuable for personalization and profiling.

Privacy Improvement Simulator

Demonstrated how small privacy-focused actions could improve overall privacy posture.

Final Verdict

Summarized overall privacy exposure and provided recommendations for improvement.

Key Learnings
Digital footprints extend beyond social media usage.
A small number of services can create a surprisingly rich digital profile.
Data visualization makes privacy risks easier to understand.
Separating facts from estimates is critical when presenting analytical insights.
Dashboard design plays an important role in communicating cybersecurity information effectively.
Privacy awareness can be improved through clear, interactive reporting.
Privacy and Ethics Considerations

This dashboard:

Does not access private databases.
Does not collect personal user information.
Uses only user-provided service data.
Clearly distinguishes between facts and estimated insights.
Avoids claiming certainty about inferred traits or behaviors.
Focuses on educational privacy awareness and responsible data analysis.

Successfully developed a premium cybersecurity-themed dashboard that visualizes digital footprint exposure, privacy risks, company influence, and potential profiling insights in an interactive and user-friendly format.
